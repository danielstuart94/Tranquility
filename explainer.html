What is the Urban Tranquillity Index?

The Tqind score predicts how pleasant a spot will feel outdoors in the next 24 hours.

How it’s calculated

We combine seven factors, normalised to 0–100 and weighted:

  • Noise 22%
  • Crowd 14%
  • Shade/microclimate 14%
  • Greenery 16%
  • Thermal comfort 12%
  • Air quality 12%
  • Amenities 10% (benches, cover, seating, water)

Scores group into Poor <45, Fair 45–59, Good 60–74, Great ≥75.

Why it matters

People stay out longer and visit more often when a place is calm, comfy, and useful. Tqind helps councils, landlords, and operators measure that and improve it.

What improves a score

Reducing traffic noise, adding shade and planting, better seating and shelter, and managing footfall at peaks.
